Did you know?

The human infant is a native of the mother’s body. A mother provides the home for her baby to form and grow, the birth path into this world, AND she is the sole source of all nutrition needed to start life outside of the womb. YOU were beautifully and wonderfully made to do amazing things!
Here in Hawaii, waiu is the word for milk. A mother’s waiu is all her infant needs to begin a healthy, nutritious, and protected life. At approximately 6 months of life, the infant begins an interest in solid foods, and the provisions of mother’s milk continues until the two mutually decide to wean. Our bodies were designed to nourish and nurture our babies! So why is it not always easy, or comfortable? Why do some moms have enough milk to feed the village, and others are struggling to produce enough for one? What can dads do to help? How or why should some moms use a pump?
These are good questions for a Lactation Consultant...

What is an IBCLC?

An IBCLC is an International Board Certified Lactation Consultant, a certification backed by evidence-based breastfeeding knowledge, and a governing body of professionals in the field. Native Nursing’s IBCLC can assist breastfeeding families in many ways but include some of the most common concerns of:

  • Positioning and latch
  • Comfort of nipples/breasts
  • Milk supply
  • Hand expression, pumping, return to work
  • Plugged ducts, engorgement, mastitis
  • Infant weight and growth
  • Preterm Infants
  • Twins/multiples

About the Lactation Consultant

Janel Aolani Takasaki, RN, BSN, IBCLC

Born and raised here in Hawaii, Janel learned about ola (health/life) in both the Hawaiian and Western models of health care. She is a Registered Nurse who obtained her Bachelors degree in Science and Nursing and started her nursing career in 1999 with the Native Hawaiian Health Care System of Oahu. After moving to a Baby Friendly hospital setting and working with the mother/baby dyad, Janel decided to specialize in breastfeeding and became an International Board Certified Lactation Consultant in 2009. Her expertise includes assisting breastfeeding families in both the hospital Labor and Delivery, Postpartum, and Neonatal Intensive Care settings as well as in the clinic and community settings. Now in Private Practice, she sees clients in the comfort of their own homes and has a Lactation Office in Kailua. This journey began with breastfeeding her own daughter Keaowena...”I was overjoyed with the birth of my daughter and the beginnings of nursing her. Then we encountered some bumps in the road. I felt like we were struggling and needed guidance, and reassurance! With the help of an IBCLC, persistence and perseverance, breastfeeding got easier and I came to enjoy not only nourishing her, but the time spent nurturing her and bonding with her. Sometimes the struggle is worth it.”
